Hello!

I'm a graduate student in the department of linguistics at the University of California, Santa Barbara, currently working on my M.A. I'm mainly interested in the ways in which queer and trans folks use language to construct identity and community, and in using computational models and methods to approach sociocultural linguistics. I'm affilliated with the [Trans Research in Linguistics Lab] under Dr. Lal Zimman, and the [Computational Psycholinguistics of Listening and Speaking Lab] under Dr. Simon Todd.

My current/ongoing projects include:

  • Examining embedded ideologies of race, place, gender, and linguistic ownership in the English pronominal constructions you guys and y'all
  • Applying computational/cognitive modeling to understand how listeners integrate acoustic information to make sexuality judgements

In 2022 I completed my B.A. at the University of California, Berkeley with majors in linguistics and computer science. While at Cal, I wrote an honors thesis on using computational models as a method for identifying acoustic variables that signal queerness, and worked as an editor on the [Gender in Language Project]. I also taught [Computer Science 61A] over eight semesters as a Tutor, TA, Head TA, and Instructor. I was an officer and president of the Society of Linguistics Undergraduate Students (affectionately known as SLUgS); was a mentor, exec, and president of Computer Science Mentors (CSM); and was a stage manager, producer, designer, and board member for the student theater company BareStage Productions.
